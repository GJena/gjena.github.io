I’m a data scientist looking to create impact


Data scientist by profession, Educator at heart

I'm a cross-portfolio Data Scientist with the Research Operations team for IBM Software. I work at the interesting intersection of User Research and Design and use data to understand users' struggles and opportunities to enhance their experiences. I graduated with a Masters in Computer Science from University of Pennsylvania in 2017.

I live in San Francisco where I spend most of my time imploring and exploring datasets using Machine Learning. I'm interested in Natural Language Processing and am fascinated by human language.

I'm extremely passionate about encouraging women in technology. I strongly support exposing children early on to Computer Science and using the superpower of coding to solve exciting and meaningful problems. I love being a part of something that delivers a change. The impact of a project serves as a huge motivator for me. If you think I might be a good fit for something, I'd love to talk!

I spend my time outside of work delivering talks and facilitating workshops. In 2019, I was fortunate enough to give more than 15 sessions at multiple conferences including PyCon US and O'Reilly's OSCON. In the past, I have taught Python at the San Francisco Public Library and also mentored high school students as part of AI4ALL. Outside of the world of bits and bytes, I'm channeling my passion for cooking (@noworryculinary), painting, writing, and occasionally pursuing my love for acting.
