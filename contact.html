I’m a data scientist looking to create impact


Data scientist by profession, Educator at heart


I am available to speak worldwide, though I usually restrict commitments outside of North America to a couple a year. I am usually in India once a year, around December, which is the ideal time for me to take up opportunities there.


My speaking experience

I started with teaching Python at the San Francisco Public Library and have since then spoken at more than 25 different conferences and events across 3 countries, most of which have been in USA. You can find a snapshot of the talks and workshops I've delivered here. I have spoken to attendees with varying backgrounds, from novices to seasoned developers, and from middle and high school students to adults. My sessions have been as small as 25 people and as large as 150 people for workshops and 400 people for talks.

I once gave a talk (to my largest audience ever) wearing a tiara and sparkly clothes. The idea was inspired by this tweet and was the brainchild of our brilliant track organizer, Michelle Casbon. All the track speakers wore sparkly clothes and tiaras because we wanted to show that one can be a scientist AND wear sparkly clothes!

Public speaking is not a part of my job responsibilities. I do this purely out of my own love and interest for teaching and speaking. I especially enjoy encouraging women and youngsters in tech. I feel lucky to have an encouraging work environment and a strong support system to be able to pursue this passion outside of my day job.

What to expect

I like to explain things in an easy-to-understand format, drawing analogies from real-life. I always aim to include elements of humor in my talks so people can relate better to what I am saying and can retain the concepts for longer. I generally use Google Slides for my presentations. Workshops involve the use of browser-based or local tools installed on computers.

Preparing the material takes weeks of work including research, writing, and creating code. I try to customize each session according to the target audience. Based on the opportunity, the speaking fee varies. At minimum, travel and accommodation are expected if the event is not local. If you aren't sure what you can offer, reach out to discuss more. Workshops are paid and depend on the topic, duration and number of attendees expected.
